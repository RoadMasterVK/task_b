By Train (DART)

www.irishrail.ie

DART (Dublin Area Rapid Transit) is a train service that connects Dublin City Center with various destinations along the coast of Dublin Bay, including Howth. Here are some key details about the DART service:

  • Route: The DART train service runs along the coastline of Dublin Bay, starting from the city center and extending northwards towards Howth. The route passes through several stations, offering scenic views of the coast and access to various coastal towns and attractions.
  • City Center Stations: The DART service in Dublin City Center operates from several stations, including:
      • - Connolly Station
      - Tara Street Station
      - Pearse Station
  • Howth Station: Howth is a picturesque village located on the Howth Peninsula in County Dublin. The DART train service terminates at Howth Station, which is conveniently located near the village center and harbor.
  • Frequency: Trains on the DART service generally run at regular intervals throughout the day, with higher frequencies during peak hours. The service is known for its reliability and punctuality.
  • Journey Duration: The journey from Dublin City Center to Howth by DART typically takes around 25 minutes, making it a quick and convenient way to reach the coastal destination.
  • Scenic Route: The DART route offers stunning views of Dublin Bay and its coastline, including beautiful beaches, cliffs, and landmarks such as Howth Head. It's a popular choice for both locals and tourists who wish to explore the coastal areas of Dublin.

    • By Bus

    www.dublinbus.ie

    Dublin Bus operates several bus routes that connect different parts of Dublin with Howth.

    To find the most convenient route and schedule, it is recommended to check with Dublin Bus directly, as they may update their routes and schedules from time to time.

    You can visit the official website of Dublin Bus or use their journey planner tool to get the latest information.

    Here are a few bus routes that commonly serve Howth:

  • Route 31/a/b: This route operates between Howth Summit and the city center, passing through Howth Village, Raheny, and Marino. The route provides access to various neighborhoods in the eastern part of Dublin.
  • Route 31d: This route connects Howth Summit with the city center, passing through Howth Village, Sutton Cross, and Clontarf. It provides an alternative option for traveling between Howth and the city center.
  • Route 102: This route runs between Sutton Station and Dublin Airport, passing through Howth Village, Baldoyle, and Portmarnock. It offers a convenient connection for those traveling to or from Howth and the airport.
  • These are just a few examples of bus routes that serve Howth. It's important to note that bus routes and schedules may vary, so it's always advisable to check with Dublin Bus for the most up-to-date information.
